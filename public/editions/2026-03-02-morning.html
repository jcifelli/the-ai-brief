Google's Gemini Can Now Do Things on Your Android Phone — Book a Ride, Order Dinner, Navigate Apps for You

TechCrunch · 9to5Google · Google Blog

Google just unlocked something that AI companies have been promising for a long time: an assistant that doesn't just answer questions, but actually does things. The new version of Gemini on Android can now handle what Google calls "multi-step tasks" — meaning you can say "order me my usual from DoorDash" and Gemini will open the app, navigate to your order, and complete the purchase, all without you touching your phone.

At launch, it works with Uber, DoorDash, and Grubhub, and it only runs on the latest hardware: Google's Pixel 10 series and Samsung's Galaxy S26. The way it works is actually clever — Gemini runs the third-party app in a secure virtual window on your phone, navigates it as if it were you, and can't access anything outside that contained window. You can watch the whole thing happen in real time and stop it if something goes wrong.

This is what the AI industry calls "agentic" AI — the idea that instead of just chatting, the AI takes action in the world on your behalf. It's something every major AI company has been working toward. Amazon's Alexa, Apple's Siri, and OpenAI's Operator product are all trying to do versions of the same thing. Google just shipped a real version of it for Android. The limitation is significant — it only works on two phone models right now — but the direction is clear: the AI on your phone is becoming something that acts, not just talks.
