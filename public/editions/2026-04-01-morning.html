Fortune · BleepingComputer · VentureBeat

Anthropic accidentally published the full source code for Claude Code — its popular AI coding assistant — in a routine software update. A 59.8 megabyte debug file was accidentally included in a new version of the software package, and it contained a link to the complete source code: nearly 2,000 files and over 500,000 lines of code.

A security researcher spotted it, posted about it publicly, and snapshots of the code were backed up in an open-source repository that has already been copied more than 41,500 times. The exposed code revealed Claude Code’s full internal architecture, unreleased features (including a “persistent assistant” that runs in the background), and internal performance data.

This happened just four days after Anthropic accidentally exposed 3,000 internal documents — including details about its secret Mythos model — through a misconfigured server. TechCrunch · VentureBeat · SiliconANGLE

Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff announced 30 new AI features for Slack, headlined by a dramatic overhaul of Slackbot. It’s the biggest upgrade since Salesforce bought Slack for $27.7 billion in 2021 — and it turns Slackbot from a simple helper into a full-fledged AI agent (a program that can take actions on your behalf, not just answer questions).

The new Slackbot, powered by Anthropic’s Claude, can listen to your meetings on Zoom, Google Meet, or any other video call by tapping into your computer’s audio. It captures what’s discussed, summarizes decisions, creates action items, and — because it’s connected to Salesforce — can update your company’s customer records automatically. It can also follow you across your entire desktop, not just inside Slack, watching what you’re working on to give you context-aware help.

Slackbot also now works as what developers call an “MCP client” — basically a connector that lets it talk to other AI tools and services across your company. On the Radar

Americans Are Using AI More Than Ever — But Trusting It Less

A new Quinnipiac University poll found that 73% of Americans have now used AI tools, up from 67% a year ago. But trust is moving in the opposite direction: only 21% say they trust AI results most of the time, 55% say AI will do more harm than good in their daily lives, and 70% believe AI will eliminate jobs — up from 56% last year. Gen Z is the most pessimistic generation about AI and jobs, which is notable since they’re also the heaviest users. Americans also overwhelmingly oppose AI data centers being built in their communities, citing electricity costs and water use.

Quinnipiac University · TechCrunch

Q1 2026 Was the Biggest Quarter for Startup Funding — Ever

Investors poured $297 billion into startups globally in Q1 2026 — up 150% year-over-year and an all-time record. AI companies captured 81% of the total. Four of the five largest venture rounds in history closed this quarter: OpenAI ($120B), Anthropic ($30B), xAI ($20B), and Waymo ($16B). U.S. companies received 83% of global funding, up from 71% a year ago. The concentration is unprecedented: those four mega-rounds alone accounted for 64% of all global venture capital spending in Q1.

OpenAI Shut Down Sora — Its AI Video Tool Was Burning $1 Million a Day

OpenAI is shutting down Sora, its AI video generator, just six months after launch. Usage collapsed from about a million users to under 500,000, while the tool was costing roughly $1 million per day to run. Disney had committed $1 billion to a partnership around Sora and reportedly found out about the shutdown less than an hour before the public announcement. The web app goes dark on April 26. It’s a rare high-profile product failure for OpenAI, and a reality check for the AI video market.
