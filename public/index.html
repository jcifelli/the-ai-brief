Lead Story

The Great Model Rush of February 2026

Sources: AI Model Rush Analysis · TechStartups · TrendForce

Seven frontier models are launching this month — Gemini 3 Pro, Sonnet 5, GPT-5.3, Qwen 3.5, GLM 5, DeepSeek v4, and Grok 4.20. Nothing like this has happened before. The gap between open and closed models is effectively gone.

When seven state-of-the-art models drop in a single month, the moat shifts from raw capability to product, distribution, and ecosystem.

Alibaba's Qwen 3.5 (released yesterday) claims to outperform GPT-5.2 and Claude Opus 4.5 on several benchmarks — and it's open-weight under Apache 2.0, supporting 201 languages. It's 60% cheaper to run than its predecessor. Meanwhile, Zhipu AI's GLM-5 was trained entirely on Huawei Ascend chips — no U.S. semiconductor hardware at all.

Three of the seven models are Chinese-developed. The U.S.-China AI competition is now playing out at the frontier model level in earnest, and the open-source ecosystem led by DeepSeek, Qwen, and Meta is delivering results at fractions of the cost of closed systems.

What Else Happened

Anthropic Raises $30B at $380B Valuation

TechCrunch · Axios · Anthropic

The Series G — led by GIC and Coatue, with D.E. Shaw, Founders Fund, and 30+ others participating — is the second-largest venture deal of all time, behind only OpenAI's $40B round in 2025. Anthropic has now raised nearly $64B total since its 2021 founding.

Revenue run-rate sits at $14B (10x annual growth for three straight years). Claude Code alone generates $2.5B+ in run-rate revenue. Customers spending $100K+ annually grew 7x last year.

So what? This is no longer a research lab raising money — it's an enterprise juggernaut. The $380B valuation puts Anthropic in the same league as the largest software companies on Earth.

Seedance 2.0 Terrifies Hollywood

TechCrunch · Futurism · NBC News

ByteDance's Seedance 2.0 dropped February 10 and went instantly viral. It generates ~20 seconds of coherent video (up from 5-8 seconds), accepts text + image + video + audio as simultaneous inputs, and produces physically plausible motion. The step up from v1 is enormous.

Then the backlash hit. Users generated Spider-Man, Darth Vader, and Baby Yoda videos. Disney, Paramount, and the Motion Picture Association all fired cease-and-desists. Screenwriter Rhett Reese posted that he's "terrified" about AI's encroachment on creative work.

So what? This is the first AI video model that genuinely scares the entertainment industry — not as a novelty, but as a production-grade threat. The copyright battle here will set precedent for years.

OpenAI Bets on Agents with Frontier Platform

TechCrunch · Fortune · OpenAI Blog

Launched February 5, Frontier is OpenAI's play to become the operating system for enterprise AI. It's vendor-agnostic (you can manage agents from Google and Anthropic too), connects to your CRM, ticketing, and data systems, and treats AI agents as first-class employees with identity and access controls.

Early adopters include Intuit, Uber, State Farm, and Thermo Fisher. One financial services firm reported getting 90% more time back for client-facing teams.

So what? OpenAI is shifting from "we sell models" to "we run your agent workforce." It's a direct response to Anthropic's Cowork and Google's enterprise relationships.

Cohere Ships Tiny Aya — AI for the Other 7 Billion

TechCrunch · AI Insider · BetaKit

Launched today at the India AI Summit, Tiny Aya is a 3.35B-parameter open-weight model family covering 70+ languages — including Bengali, Hindi, Tamil, Urdu, and many African languages. It runs offline on a laptop. Trained on just 64 H100 GPUs.

Regional variants include TinyAya-Global, TinyAya-Earth (Africa), TinyAya-Fire (South Asia), and TinyAya-Water (Asia-Pacific/Europe). Available now on HuggingFace, Kaggle, and Ollama.

So what? Most frontier AI only works well in English and a handful of other languages. This is AI that works for the majority of the world's population — offline, on cheap hardware.

Looking Inside the Black Box: Mechanistic Interpretability Hits the Big Time

MIT Technology Review · MIT (Deep Dive)

MIT Technology Review named mechanistic interpretability one of its 10 Breakthrough Technologies for 2026. The field maps internal circuits inside AI models to understand why they behave the way they do — and sometimes deceive us.

Anthropic used it in pre-deployment safety checks for Claude Sonnet 4.5. Goodfire, a startup in the space, just closed a $150M Series B at a $1.25B valuation after using interpretability methods to discover novel Alzheimer's biomarkers — the first major scientific finding from reverse-engineering a foundation model.

So what? We're building X-ray machines for AI brains. This could become the foundation for evidence-based AI regulation — and it's already producing real scientific discoveries.
